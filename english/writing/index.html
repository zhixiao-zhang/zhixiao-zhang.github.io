Writing

习惯表达

  • Granted 
                
    用于句子开头：诚然，表示让步
  • sth. in the context of sth. 
                
    在 B 的背景下 A
  • is all 
                
    仅仅是、就是，**强调**一个小事务就能产生很大的影响
e.g. A simple, innocuous bug _is all_ that it can take to turn a reasonably accurate and useful model into a misleading and incorrect mess. -- _research power tools_
  • My advice comes from **having made all of these mistakes myself**. 
                
    这句话更自然，强调个人经验，更有说服力，总是比 My advice comes from the mistakes made by me. 更好。后者更正式、生硬、少见。
与 My advice comes from the mistakes I have made myself. 相比，后者更注重强调我犯过错误，前者在此基础上更强调我犯过错误给我带来的经验。
  • It’s not a matter of if but when.
  • All that's {neccessary, required, needed} is {action, description} 
                
    可以与下面连用： something along the lines of {description} 诸如 {描述}
表示：所有要xx的就只是诸如xx的。
  • or so 
                
    大约，左右
或是表示模糊：or so the folklore goes
  • scooped 
                
    在学术或研究背景下，“scooped” 是一个术语，表示某人在研究领域中被抢先完成或发表了一项成果。
  • to add insult to injury 
                
    使伤口雪上加霜，雪上加霜的是，更糟糕的是
e.g. To add insult to injury, the company has now decided to cut our pay.
  • what have you 
                
    诸如此类的东西，等等（非正式）
e.g. I like all kinds of music: rock, pop, jazz, what have you.
  • holy trinity 
                
    经常用来表示三个重要的事物、概念或原则，比如我在做某件事时使用的三个重要工具。
  • have yet to 
                
    尚未
  • err on the side of 
                
    宁愿过于；偏向于，倾向于
e.g. I always err on the side of caution when I'm driving in the rain.

复杂句子分析（用于模仿）

After incorporating this “chained” feedback, your paper will end up much stronger than it would have, had you gotten feedback on only a single revision of your paper.

“When first presented with a new data set, we typically seek to understand the data on a high level.”

这里省略了主语，we are

“By no means do you need to use Twitter to be successful in your field”

句首的 “By no means” 是一个否定副词短语，用于强调完全没有必要。

当否定副词位于句首时，句子需要部分倒装（将助动词提前）。

使用 “By no means” 而不是简单的 “You don’t need”，显得更有说服力，同时语气也更正式一些。

类似的否定副词还有：

  • Under no circumstances
  • At no time

当不需要强烈反驳时应使用正向表达，如：is not required to, etc.
